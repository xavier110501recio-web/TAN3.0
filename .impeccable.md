# Design Context

## Users
Aspiring entrepreneurs with a business idea or income goal who don't know where to start. They check in daily, often on mobile, often during a small window (morning coffee, lunch break, before bed). They want a single clear next move — not plans, not theory, not pep talks.

## Brand Personality
Voice: brutal honesty + genuine care. Direct, forward-facing, slightly irreverent, warm but uncompromising. Three-word personality: **honest, structured, unromantic**. Emotional goal: respect, not motivation. The user should feel taken seriously, never coddled.

## Aesthetic Direction
**Editorial × Brutalist field manual.** Magazine-grade typography (large Bodoni serif headlines, asymmetric layouts, generous whitespace, mono folios, pull quotes) crossed with workshop-manual rawness (hairline rules instead of cards, mono labels, sharp edges, visible grid structure, exposed bones). Dark + gold + cream palette stays. Mobile-first; desktop reads as a 2-column spread.

Anti-references: rounded-corner-everything dashboards, glassy SaaS gradients, neon-on-dark crypto aesthetics, AI-default cyan-purple, generic motivational-app brightness.

## Design Principles
1. **Hairlines over cards.** Sections divide with 1px rules. Cards only where there's an affordance (button, input, surface that holds an action).
2. **Mono labels, serif headlines.** The folio/mono strips do the orientation work; the Bodoni does the gravitas.
3. **Gold is rare and earned.** Mission numbers, active states, primary CTAs. Never decorative.
4. **The mission card is the centerpiece.** Every other surface defers to it.
5. **Adapt, don't shrink.** Desktop becomes a magazine spread, not a phone in a frame.
